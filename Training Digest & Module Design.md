## 1. COACHING FRAMEWORKS

### Play-Practice-Play (Foundation of Football Australia)
The overarching session philosophy. Always start and end with a game.

| Phase | Time | Coach's Role | Purpose |
|-------|------|-------------|---------|
| **PLAY** (start) | ~15 min | Influence — shape the theme subtly, introduce challenges | Build 1v1 → 2v1 → 3v3 as players arrive |
| **PRACTICE** | 3 × 10 min | Teach — break down technique, allow repetition | Fun inclusive practice games |
| **PLAY** (end) | ~15 min | Observe — check transfer of learning, positive reinforcement | Bring skills back to the game |

### SO CHANGE IT (the coaching adjustment tool)
When something's not working, change one element:
- **S** — Safe
- **O** — Observe
- **C** — Coaching Styles
- **H** — How to Score
- **A** — Area (bigger/smaller)
- **N** — Numbers (more/fewer players)
- **G** — Game Rules
- **E** — Equipment
- **I** — Inclusion
- **T** — Time

### BE G.O.O.D (Miniroos session design)
Always start and end with play:
- **G** — Goals
- **O** — Opponents
- **O** — Opportunities
- **D** — Direction

### CREATE T.O.E.S (Miniroos self-reflection checklist)
After every session, coach asks:
- **T** — Touches of the ball (did every player touch it often?)
- **O** — Organisation (was the session well run?)
- **E** — Enjoyment (were kids having fun?)
- **S** — Safety (was it safe?)

### CHANGE IT (ALDI MiniRoos / Game Sense version of SO CHANGE IT)
Same concept, slightly different framing:
- **C** — Coaching Style
- **H** — How to Score
- **A** — Area
- **N** — Numbers
- **G** — Game Rules
- **E** — Equipment
- **I** — Inclusion
- **T** — Time

### Game Sense Principles (ALDI MiniRoos)
- The game IS the teacher — use well-designed games, not drills
- Coach = facilitator, not instructor
- Success criteria: laughter, all involved, all achieving something, 50–100 ball contacts per player

## 2. AGE GROUP FORMATS

### MiniRoos U6 & U7
- 4-a-side, no goalkeeper, max 3 subs
- Field: 30m × 20m | Goal: 2m × 1m | Ball: Size 3
- Duration: 20 min halves | No offside | No throw-ins (dribble/pass in) | No corners
- No points tables published

### MiniRoos U8 & U9
- 7-a-side including GK, max 4 subs
- Field: 40–50m × 30–40m | Goal: 3m × 2m | Ball: Size 3
- Duration: 20 min halves | No offside | Throw-ins apply | Corners apply
- GK can't kick/drop-kick from hands; must roll or play from ground within 6 seconds
- No points tables published

### MiniRoos U10 & U11
- 9-a-side including GK, max 5 subs
- Field: 60–70m × 40–50m | Goal: 5m × 2m | Ball: Size 4
- Duration: 25 min halves | Offside not enforced (teach it, don't penalise it)
- Same GK rules as U8/U9
- No points tables published

### 9v9 Match Format (U10+, UQFC)
Substitution every 8 minutes across positional groups:
- Attackers: 4 in squad, 3 on field, 1 bench
- Midfielders: 3 in squad, 2 on field, 1 bench
- Defenders: 5 in squad, 4 on field (incl. GK), 1 bench
- GK1 plays full first half, GK2 plays full second half; they rotate in the opposite half in the defender group
- Match blocks: 0–8 | 8–16 | 16–24 | HT | 25–33 | 33–41 | 41–49

## 3. UQFC SESSION LIBRARY

### Session Structure (all topics follow this format)
Every UQFC session is ~70-75 minutes:
1. **Rondo (20 min)** — Small-area passing/possession game with defenders. Includes 2 × 30-second Dual Force (physical challenge) or Speed Agility between sets.
2. **Positioning Game (25 min)** — Larger area, theme-specific game with neutrals, end zones, or position roles.
3. **Small Sided Game (25-30 min)** — 4v4 or 5v5 with king-of-court or tournament rules; theme carried into the game.
4. **Water breaks** throughout (4–6 min each).

### Dual Force / Speed Agility (standard warmup games)
Recurring mini-games played between rondo sets (30 sec each):
- Push Offs, Hop n Pull, Knights n Horses, Arm Wrestle, Back Pushes, Gang Up, King of the Ring, Chain Race, Agility Square, Tag games

---

### TOPIC 1 — Pressing
**Principle:** Win the ball back as quickly as possible, as close to the goal as possible.
**Behaviour:** Sprint after the ball immediately.
**Moments when to press:**
- After a turnover in the attacking or midfield third
- On the opponent's first touch under pressure
- When the opposition's back is to goal

**Key coaching points:**
- Closest player sprints to the ball immediately
- Other players prevent forward play first, then anticipate where the next pass goes
- Work together — stay connected, close the gaps
- Don't get split or played around; compact as a unit
- Man-mark press: 1 player per attacker

**Session 1 — Rondo 4v2:**
- 10×8m rectangle, 3×4 min sets with Dual Force between
- Defenders hold bibs; drop when they retain ball or are closest to it when kicked out
- Progression: defenders must steal and dribble out; attackers need 8 passes then chip/catch

**Session 1 — Pressing Game 4v4v4:**
- 15×20m, 3 teams; one team presses for 60 seconds then swaps
- Progression: double points for scoring within 8 seconds of winning possession

**Session 2 — Pressing Rondo 3v3v3 (25×15m):**
- 2 teams attack, 1 defends; defenders earn 3 pts per pass completed when they win the ball
- Speed agility tag game between sets

**Session 2 — Half Field Game:**
- Red team scores by driving into end zone or finding neutral
- Blue scores in big goal; swap neutral team every 2–3 min
- Medium game: 5+GK v 5+GK on half pitch

---

### TOPIC 2 — Counter Attacking
**Principle:** Break forward quickly after winning the ball.
**Behaviour:** Flood with numbers, attack at speed together.
**Moments:**
- After stealing ball from opposition attack/set play
- When recovering from a defensive position
- After chasing opposition in their build-out

**Key coaching points:**
- Flood forward with numbers immediately
- Attack with width and depth; always 1 player back to prevent opposition counter
- Create 2v1 overloads; use overlaps and underlaps to make defenders change lines
- Speed of transition — encourage flood moment

**Session 1 — Speed Rondo 4v1+1 Transfer:**
- Two rectangular areas; attackers must transfer ball to the other side
- Progression: receiving player must play first-time back to a supporting attacker

**Session 1 — Counter Game (escalating):**
- Starts 1v1, escalates to 2v1, 3v2, 3v3, 4v3... up to 5v5, then back down
- If defenders steal, they counter the other way; 5 min sets

---

### TOPIC 3 — Building Out
**Principle:** Build from the back; drag opposition out of position and play forward.
**Behaviour:** Exploit the space created by drawing opponents in.
**Moments:**
- When GK has possession
- Building out from the defensive third

**Key coaching points:**
- Always scan: where is the defender? Where are teammates? Where is the space?
- Play around and through the defender
- Reposition to find the ball; connect the neutrals
- Receive between neutrals to give time to play through
- Motivate defensive recovery if ball is lost in build-up

**Session 1 — Rondo 3v1 (8×5m):**
- 6×90 sec sets; defender drops bib when retaining ball
- Progression: 10 passes and catch keeps defender in

**Session 1 — 2v2+2 to EndZone (20×15m):**
- Attacking team works ball from neutral to neutral for a point
- Defending team can play to the neutral for a point when they win it
- Progression: first-time pass = double points

---

### TOPIC 4 — Playing Through Midfield
**Principle:** Play through or around opposition to progress the ball forwards.
**Behaviour:** Find space, get into passing lanes, play forward.
**Moments:**
- As a defender drives into midfield
- When in possession in the midfield third

**Key coaching points:**
- "Rope analogy" — we're all connected; defenders are scissors trying to cut the rope; constantly reposition yourself to receive the ball and play your next pass
- Correct body profile to receive and play forward quickly
- Sequential thought: **Over → Through → Beside → Behind**
- Look for the furthest pass first; check down if not available

**Session 1 — Rondo 4v2/4v1 Diamond (10×10m):**
- Standard rondo with diamond shape; focus on finding space inside
- Progression: 10 passes + catch to keep defenders in

**Session 1 — 4v4+4 Positioning Game (25×20m):**
- Play from one neutral to another for a point
- End players reposition along the full end line

**Session 1 — Training Game 5v5, 2-1-2 setup:**
- Play through the central yellow zone scores

---

### TOPIC 5 — Getting in Behind
**Principle:** Play through or over the defence; exploit the space behind.
**Behaviour:** Look for runs into space; attack depth and width.
**Moments:**
- When in middle or attacking third
- When the opposition has left space in behind

**Key coaching points:**
- Constantly scan where the furthest pass can go
- Play a pass that allows the receiver to control comfortably
- Move the ball to move defenders to create gaps
- Key directions: **Behind, Beside, Through, Over**
- Arrive in space on time; adjust distances

**Session 1 — Rondo 4v2 Diamond (10×10m):**
- Normal rondo; focus on playing through/over defenders

**Session 1 — Cross the River (3-zone positioning game):**
- Team must play through or over the defending zone to the other side
- Progression: defenders can send in as many as they like

---

### TOPIC 6 — Final Third (Finishing)
**Principle:** Creative decision making in the attacking third.
**Behaviour:** Be ruthless — create and take goal-scoring opportunities.
**Moments:**
- Entering the attacking third
- Delivering into the opposition's box

**Key coaching points:**
- Speed of thought: perceive and execute quickly
- Disguise intentions before executing
- What do we need to score? Clear shooting line
- If no shooting line: find a clear passing line to score
- Drive players to shoot and finish off every ball
- Reinforce defensive actions too (realism)

**Session 1 — Rondo 4v2 Team Competition (16×10m):**
- Attackers score in mini goal; defenders dribble beyond top line
- Progression: first-time finish = double points; offside in rondo

**Session 1 — 4v4+4 Positioning Game (servers/attackers/defenders):**
- Servers have balls and play to attackers to score
- Defenders win ball and play through a gate
- Attackers can't play back to servers

---

### TOPIC 7 — Recovery Runs
**Principle:** Organise defence behind the ball quickly.
**Behaviour:** Sprint back behind the ball as a unit immediately after losing possession.
**Moments:**
- When opposition plays long down the pitch
- When opposition is in our half

**Key coaching points:**
- Sprint back behind the ball immediately after losing possession
- Recover together with your teammate
- Compact and prevent forward play
- Do NOT coach during the rondo; max 30 seconds between sets

**Session 1 — Recovery Rondo 4v2 Transfer (20×10m):**
- Attackers must play through to the other side; always have 3v2 in the area
- Progression: player not in square must play first-time; defenders get back behind ball before chasing

**Session 1 — Shooting Game (Recovery Runs):**
- Starts 3v1; when ball goes out, resting players join to create 3v1 the other way
- Becomes 3v2 after 2 rounds

---

### TOPIC 8 — Defensive Block
**Principle:** Compact defensive shape, wait patiently, then steal the ball.
**Behaviour:** Reduce space, be patient, win the ball at the right moment.
**Moments:**
- When opposition ball/player enters our half
- As opposition enters our defensive third

**Key coaching points:**
- Prevent forward play: where can we direct the opposition? How can we delay?
- Trap the opposition into predictable positions to win the ball
- Don't leave the ground; be patient
- "If we know we can win the ball, be crazy and go win it"

**Session 1 — Rondo 4v2 10×8m:**
- Round 1: defender touches ball = becomes attacker
- Round 2: defender must steal and dribble outside
- Round 3: attackers can steal back if defender tries to dribble out

**Session 1 — 2v2+2 Positioning Game (25×15m):**
- Attackers try to work ball from one side to another
- Defenders win ball and play to neutral
- Progression: force attackers to play back to same neutral = point

**Session 1 — Small Game Tournament:**
- 4 teams, World Cup format; 3 pts win, 1 pt draw, bonus for clean sheet

---

### TOPIC 9 — Adapting
**Principle:** Adjust to the opponent or situation to control game moments.
**Behaviour:** Read the game; change strategy based on context.
**Moments:**
- When we need to control the game
- When we need to adapt to influence the outcome

**Key coaching points:**
- Question players on actions they'd change based on different rules
- Players must adjust angles, distance, timing, and lines
- Ask questions — don't give commands
- Interventions max 10 seconds in the game

**Session 1 — Rondo (3 rounds of different rules):**
- Round 1: 30 passes + catch to keep defender; split passes worth more points
- Round 2: 2 teams, each sends invaders to other rondo
- Round 3: 3 teams, defending team can place any number of defenders in either square

**Session 1 — Game 6v6 Scenario Sets:**
- Set 1: "10 min to go, your team is ahead" — coach the defensive side
- Set 2: "10 min to go, need 2 goals to win" — coach the attacking side
- Set 3: "Nil-nil, decide to defend or attack"
- Set 4: Normal game

---

## 6. PROPOSED TRAINING MODULE — APP DESIGN

### Module Goal
Give the coach a session planner, drill library, and training log — all themed around the UQFC and FQ frameworks used by the club.

---

1. **Plan a Session** — Browse topics and create a session plan
2. **My Training Log** — View past session records

Filter by:
- **Age Group**: MiniRoos (U6–U11) | Junior (U9–U13) | Senior (U14–U18)
- **Focus Area**: Attacking | Defending | Transition | Technical | Adapting

Topic cards in a grid (9 topics for U9-U13):

| Topic | Category | Icon |
|-------|----------|------|
| Pressing | Defending | 🔴 |
| Defensive Block | Defending | 🛡️ |
| Recovery Runs | Defending | 🔄 |
| Building Out | Attacking | 🏗️ |
| Playing Through Midfield | Attacking | ➡️ |
| Getting in Behind | Attacking | 🚀 |
| Final Third | Attacking | ⚽ |
| Counter Attacking | Transition | ⚡ |
| Adapting | Tactical | 🧠 |
| Restarting | Set Pieces | 🎯 |

### Screen C: Session Detail
When a topic is tapped, shows:

**Header:** Topic name, principle, behaviour, moments (when to use it)

**3 Phase blocks (collapsible):**

```
▶ RONDO (20 min)
  Setup: Area, numbers, duration
  Rules: ...
  Progressions: ...
  Dual Force: [mini challenge]
  Coaching points: ...

▶ POSITIONING GAME (25 min)
  Setup: ...
  Rules: ...
  Coaching points: ...

▶ SMALL SIDED GAME (25-30 min)
  Setup: ...
  Rules: ...
  Coaching points: ...
```

**Footer:** "Use This Session" button → opens Session Logger

---

### Screen D: Session Logger
Pre-filled from the selected session topic. Coach fills in:
- Date (auto-fills today)
- Attendance (number of players)
- Session focus (pre-filled from topic)
- What went well (text area)
- What to improve (text area)
- Player notes (optional individual notes)
- Rating: 1–5 ⭐ for T.O.E.S (Touches, Organisation, Enjoyment, Safety)

Save → goes to Training Log.

---

### Screen E: Training Log
List of past sessions (most recent first):
- Date | Topic | Attendance | T.O.E.S rating
- Tap to expand for full notes

## 8. KEY DESIGN NOTES FOR THE APP

- **Keep it practical for game day coaches** — the main use case is "I need to plan Tuesday training in 5 minutes"
- **Don't drown in text** — show coaching points as bullet-point chips, collapsible sections
- **T.O.E.S rating** should be prominent — it's the FQ-endorsed self-reflection tool the club uses
- **Link to match day** — if a team has been leaking goals, suggest Recovery Runs or Defensive Block sessions
- **Dual Force games** should be shown as fun mini-challenges — kids love them, and coaches forget them
- **Progression toggles** — coach can mark a progression as "done" during a session to track what they've covered
- **Age group context** — the app already knows which team the coach is managing, so default to the right age group
